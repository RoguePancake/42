## Nuclear Balance in Game

### The Player's 1 Nuke Should:
- **Deter invasion**: AI gets -40% war willingness against nuclear nations
- **Be a one-shot asset**: Once used, deterrence is gone forever
- **Create diplomatic events**: Disarmament pressure, sharing offers, proliferation crises
- **Be targetable**: City-buster (destroys 1 city completely) OR army-killer (destroys 1 army stack)
- **Have delivery risk**: 70% chance of hitting target (can be intercepted)
- **NOT make player invincible**: Conventional forces still needed for offense

### Ashfall's Uranium:
- Ashfall has the resources to eventually build nukes (80 uranium)
- This creates a "proliferation race" — AI nations may try to conquer Ashfall before they arm up
- Player can trade uranium to Ashfall for alliance, or help conquer them for the resources

## Custom Nation: Geography-to-Resources Formula

When the player places their capital, scan a 50-tile radius and derive:

```
coastalRatio = coastal_tiles / total_tiles
mountainRatio = mountain_tiles / total_tiles
forestRatio = forest_tiles / total_tiles
grassRatio = grass_tiles / total_tiles
sandRatio = sand_tiles / total_tiles

Iron       = 10 + (mountainRatio × 60) + (hillsRatio × 40)
Oil        = 5 + (sandRatio × 50) + (coastalRatio × 30)
Uranium    = 5 + (mountainRatio × 30) + (sandRatio × 20)
Electronics = 10 + (grassRatio × 20)   // need developed land
Manpower   = 10 + (grassRatio × 40) + (forestRatio × 30)
Food       = 10 + (grassRatio × 50) + (forestRatio × 20) + (coastalRatio × 15)
```

Treasury = 800 + (coastalRatio × 500) + (grassRatio × 300)

This means:
- **Coastal capital** → oil-rich, food bonus, higher treasury
- **Mountain capital** → iron/uranium-rich, defensible, poor food
- **Forest capital** → manpower-rich, decent food, concealment
- **Plains capital** → food breadbasket, high manpower
- **Desert capital** → oil/uranium, harsh, low everything else
